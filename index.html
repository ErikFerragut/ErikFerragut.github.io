Erik M. Ferragut is an Applied Research Mathematician and a Cyber Security Research Scientist in the Cybserspace Sciences and Information Intelligence Research Group within the Oak Ridge National Laboratory. He has applied probabilistic modeling and mathematical methods to diverse problems in support of the Department of Defense and the Department of Homeland Security, developing scalable machine learning solutions for data fusion and analytics. His current research focuses on the use of probabilistic models for unsupervised anomaly detection and heterogeneous data analysis in cyber security and machine learning. Previously, he worked as a cryptologic researcher for over 10 years. Erik earned his Ph.D. in Mathematics from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor in 2003 (just hours before the Northeast blackout).

Research Interests

My primary interest is in the application of probabilistic modeling to problems in unsupervised machine learning. This has generally focused on large scale anomaly detection. In addition to anomaly detection, I am currently working on probabilistic schema matching, resilient control theory, quantum computation, and, you know, whatever.

Other Interests

I also fill two additional roles:
  1. I am the team lead for the Cyber Defenders Team, the team charged with transferring cyber research technologies to practice within ORNL's IT system.
  2. I am the Director of the Cyber Security Institute, an annual 10-week research initiative where talented students are invited to work on real-world problems in cyber security.
